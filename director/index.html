In His Own Words

A terrible and grandiose masterpiece of horror. That's the vision of FERTILITY. I'm ready to make it. Here's how we got here.

I started in a room by myself. Writing, directing, shooting, editing, designing the sets. Salvation was a film made with no crew, no budget, and no excuse not to make it. You learn fast when every department is your problem.

Cherrypicker proved I could work with actors. Colin Mochrie taught me something on that set: comedy and horror live in the same room. The audience laughing is the audience with their guard down. That's when you hit them.

The Christmas Spirit proved something else: a good director can create his own genre. I took a Christmas movie and refused to follow any of the usual formulas. Original story, original tone, original rules. When you bend a genre to your voice, you're not a hired gun. You're a filmmaker.

Raptus was the machine at work. Apple TV worldwide, German theatrical, international distribution, real money on the line. Sci-fi on the surface. Domestic horror underneath.

Every film has been bigger, darker, more technically demanding than the last. Every film has delivered.

FERTILITY is the most ambitious thing I've written. Intelligent. Monstrous. Wide audience potential. 50% financed. Cast-ready script.
